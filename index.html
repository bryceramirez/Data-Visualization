HIV

in the Philippines

HIV stands for immunodeficiency virus.

HIV is considered to be one of the world's most serious public health and social problems.

This could reduce the number of CD4 cells which makes the body unable to fight off infections and diseases such as cancer.

AIDS is the most severe type of HIV infection which happens after the final stage of HIV.

As of September 2019, there are a total of 71,778 HIV cases in the Philippines. A total of 3,617 have died.

In 2018, the Philippines has been considered as the country with the fastest growing HIV epidemic in the world by the United Nations Program. However, people still remain silent or are too shy to talk about it.

HIV Statistics 2016 - 2019

Total Reported Cases Per Month

The number of these stories increase every month as the years progress. The lowest was from April 2017 with 629 cases or stories while the highest was in January 2019 with 1249. From these stories of HIV cases, a few of which would translate to AIDS cases.

Cases of AIDS

Nonetheless, these stories increase through the years too. March 2019 was the highest having 215 cases. Lowest was from December 2016 with 72 cases.

Age Group Distribution

These stories of Jose Marie range from all ages as well. Most of these occur at adulthood ranging from 25-34 years old, followed by cases from teenagers at ages around 15 to 24. Older stories follow this such as the range of 35 to 49 and 50 years of age and above. The least stories reported were from children that aged below 15 years old.

Gender Distribution

There are more stories of Jose Maries that are male, compared to those that are female. The highest cases from each sex were from 2018. Males had 10,862 cases while female cases were at 599.

Monthly Deaths

At times, their stories conclude a tragic end. The deaths caused by HIV also increases each year as presented. The highest amount of which occurred in April 2019 which recorded a total of 189 deaths.

Testing Center Distribution

Stories or Jose Marie or HIV cases are told at testing centers which contain the necessary equipment to diagnose such disease. With this, most of the testing centers belong to the Luzon Island Group of the Philippines. Among all the regions, NCR, contains the highest number of testing center hospitals with an amount of 122. Least of which is region 2 with 2 testing centers.

Cases per Region

Similar with the testing center distribution, most of these cases occur at NCR having the highest amount of distribution. This is followed by regions 4A, 7, 3 and 11 with significant number of cases in recent years.

Though these stories are told, they aren’t really heard by everyone else. These are not understood appropriately by everyone. Most of us think about them as tales of horror causing us to fear them. We build a wall around them, creating stigmas and promoting discrmination in workplaces, learning institutions, hospitals, and in public.

But these are not stories of horror and terror, these are tales of tragedy; a cry for help.

We fear what we do not understand. There are a lot of misconceptions about HIV that greatly hinders the cure for various cases. As we fear them, they fear themselves too. A lot of Jose Maries become afraid of getting tested due to the fear of being shunned away.

Instead, the right way is for us to be educated, and reach out to these stories. The conservative tradition of Filipinos to view HIV makes matters worse. The silence and unsympathetic stance for these stories create more harm for these stories. What Jose Marie needs are more testing centers, access to knowledge, and proper treatment.
